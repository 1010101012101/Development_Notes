Electronics Workshops this November
Welcome to the Electronics workshops taking place this November. Are young interested in or currently learning electronic engineering? Have you been learning Arduino and have some ideas for cool applications? Then get more experience and learn how to do (almost) anything with small but powerful microcontroller. We will be running a series of workshops diving into embedded microcontroller development. Going from Blinking LED's and "Hello world" to interrupt-driven, power saving, advanced 32-bit MCU development. Practice developing and prototyping industry standard microcontrollers such as STM32, PSoC4, or the well-familiar Atmel ATmega. Once we are comfortable and familiar with the insides of the Microcontroller we will begin prototyping interactive embedded electronics with Bluetooth-modules, ultra-sonic proximity sensors, universal and stepper motors and PIR motion sensors. (If this is too easy for you we can go a step further and look at using Raspberry Pi or Android to jump into the Internet of Things. ) The group size will be small so that we can go as deep into topic as possible. In order to team you up into matching groups please fill out the feedback forms ASAP... First come first served!
